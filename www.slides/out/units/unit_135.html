Colour Concepts and Colour Words

At this point you might wonder what is happening when children first start using colour words.
If you ask them about the colour of something, they will give you a colour word.
So they do know something about colour.
But they use colour words at random.
So we can't plausibly think of them as mapping colour words to concepts they already have.

How do children use colour words before they use them correctly?

Let's suppose that Bloom is right.
What's interesting about Kowalski and Zimiles is that they show that knowing some colour words seems
to come \emph{before} having colour concepts.

‘two things are involved in knowing the meaning of a word—having the concept and mapping the concept onto the right form’

\citep[p. \ 17]{Bloom:2000qz}

(Bloom 2000, p. 17)
